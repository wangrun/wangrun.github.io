Research Interests

      My research interests are at the intersection of computer security and AI. Specifically, most of our research aims at enhancing the security and trustworthiness of machine learning models, fighting the misuse of AI, like DeepFakes, and securing low-level software and systems security. Recently, my primary research are as follows.

  • AI Security. I explore vulnerabilities of machine learning systems, endeavor to enhance the security and trustworthiness of machine learning models, fight against the misuse of AI, like DeepFakes.

    • DeepFake Attack and Defense (e.g., images, voices, videos, and texts)

    • Adversarial Example Attack and Defense

    • DNN backdoor, watermarking

  • Software and Systems Security. I study the security and privacy issues caused by vulnerabilities and malware by employing program analysis and AI techniques in securing software and systems.

  • Data-driven Security. I am interested in adopting machine learning, natural language processing techniques to address the security and privacy issues driven by massive system data, network data, application data, etc.
