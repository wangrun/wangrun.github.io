About Me

  • I am currently a postdoctoral research fellow working with Prof. LIU Yang at Cyber Security Lab of Nanyang Technological University (NTU), Singapore, since Apr. 2019. My research interests are at the intersection of computer security and AI. Specifically, most of our research aims at securing low-level software and systems security and enhancing the security and trustworthiness of machine learning models.

Research Interests

     Recently, my primary research focus on Software and Systems Security, Data-driven Security, and AI Security.

  • Software and Systems Security. I study the security and privacy issues caused by vulnerabilities and malware by employing program analysis and AI techniques in securing software and systems.

  • Data-driven Security. I am interested in adopting machine learning, natural language processing techniques to address the security and privacy issues driven by massive system data, network data, application data, etc.

  • AI Security. I explore vulnerabilities of machine learning systems and endeavor to enhance the security and trustworthiness of machine learning models. Additionally, I am also interested in developing tools against the abuse of AI, like DeepFake.
